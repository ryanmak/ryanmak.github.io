iPod Modding (Ongoing)

Inspired by a really funny YouTube channel, I wanted to try and mod an iPod myself and make it look unique. After finding an iPod Video (5th generation) on the FaceBook Marketplace, I made a list of goals I wanted to acomplish when modding my iPod:

  • Add a Flash mod by replacing the Hard Drive (yes, these iPods had tiny spinning hard drives in them) with an SD card.
  • Replace the old battery with a 2500mah or greater capacity.
  • Replace the front and back plates of the iPod with unique colours.
  • Add a bluetooth module without sacrificing any core functionality (ie headphone jack).

3D Modelling and Printing

I was first introduced to 3D modelling in a highschool class called Tech Design. This class would eventually be given a couple of 3D printers for students to use, and I was immediately hooked. Since then, it was always in the back of my mind that 3D printers were only getting cheaper and that I would get one when the time was right. In my final year of university, I bought an Anet A8 3D printer from AliExpress and loved it. After a few months of use, I then upgraded to an Ender 3 and still use this printer to this day.

Autodesk Inventor was still my tool of choice, and by using my student email I got the education version to play around with. Finding a website called Thingiverse, I started uploading my designs and also downloaded other designs shared on the platform.
